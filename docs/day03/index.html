Exercises for Day 03

  • [55min] Processing ChIP-seq datasets: from A to Z with Nextflow workflow manager: [Rmd]

    Data for this part of the course was collected from multiple research papers, in which ChIP-seq (or variants of) experiments have been conducted in Yeast, in normal conditions (no specific treatment).
    In this practical, we will process multiple datasets from different experiments, using a unified pipeline.

    • An efficient targeted nuclease strategy for high-resolution mapping of DNA binding sites (Skene and Henikoff, eLife 2017)
    • Molecular mechanisms of ribosomal protein gene coregulation (Reja et al., Genes Dev. 2015)
    • Fine-Resolution Mapping of TF Binding and Chromatin Interactions (Gutin et al., Cell Rep. 2016)
    • Widespread and precise reprogramming of yeast protein–genome interactions in response to heat shock (Vinayachandran et al., Genome Res. 2018)
    • Hog1 Controls Global Reallocation of RNA Pol II upon Osmotic Shock in Saccharomyces cerevisiae (Cook et al., G3 2012)

  • [55min] Motif enrichment analysis with MEME workflow: [Rmd]

    Motifs enriched in TF-occupied loci can be identified using motif enrichment tools such as the MEME suite .
    Here, we will try to identify putative DNA sequence motifs that may be recognized by each factor studied in this practical.

  • [55min] Aggregating ChIP-seq results: [Rmd]

    Depending on the biological question, ChIP-seq results can be integrated to test hypotheses and uncover biological insights.
    Here, we will focus on identifying potential interactions between the different transcription factors analyzed in this course.
